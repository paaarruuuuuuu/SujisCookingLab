Beginning of Cooking Lab

A dear friend of mine had started to post pictures in her social networking site of food that she had cooked, and I was greatly intrigued by the whole concept of it. I also happened to see many others do something similar & I thought I'd try it too. I looked at their recipes, and once again, I took easy ones & tried to recreate a dish that looked & tasted as delicious as theirs. I took snaps of those that managed to do well, and posted them in my profile. Since I was experimenting with various ingredients & procedures, my kitchen turned into a cooking lab. I was only able to post photos of my successful attempts, but could not post their recipes as well. So when my friends & my hubby suggested that my dishes were blog worthy, I decided to begin one.

So for those who are visiting my blog for the first time, a hearty welcome from me. I'd be glad to hear from you, so please share your views & opinions with me (encouraging & pleasant ones pleeezzz).

Pro Tips & Tricks

For making soft chapathis

To get really soft chapathis, the trick lies in the kneading. The more you knead, the smoother the dough becomes and the smoother the dough is, the softer the chapathis. Another secret to soft chapathis is using fresh yoghurt or milk instead of water, to make the dough!

If your curry gets too salty

Peel and cut a large potato in half and add it to your curry. It will absorb the extra salt.

Making cream for gravies instantly

Take 3/4th cup of milk, 1/3rd cup of butter and mix well. You'll get 1 cup of cream.

Storing lemon

If you require only half of a lemon while cooking, you can store the remaining half for a few days in the fridge by applying a little salt on the cut portion.

Frying potatoes

While frying potatoes for chips or fries, apply a little maida on the potatoes to get a golden brown colour.
